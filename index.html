Rules & Guidelines

As a Rotary Youth Exchange student, you must adhere to specific rules designed to ensure your safety and the success of your exchange. Remember: No matter your age, you are considered a minor under this program.

The 4 Ds Rule

  • No Drinking of alcoholic beverages (except under host family supervision in their home only)
  • No Dating or romantic attachments (sexual relations forbidden)
  • No Drugs (except those prescribed by a physician)
  • No Driving of any motorized vehicles (cars, motorcycles, boats, etc.)

Personal Conduct

  • No body piercing or tattoos during exchange
  • No smoking (legal age in Thailand is 18)
  • No illegal downloads from the internet
  • No immodest clothing or behavior
  • Modest social media use (Facebook, WhatsApp, etc.)

Travel Restrictions

  • Submit travel permission request 3 weeks prior to travel date
  • No traveling alone or with only other students
  • No overseas travel during exchange
  • Always travel with proper adult chaperones
  • Travel during school sessions is discouraged

Family & Social

  • 30-day communication blackout with home upon arrival
  • Parent visits: Only during last 3 months of exchange
  • Always inform host family of whereabouts
  • Be reachable at all times via mobile phone
  • Attend host Rotary Club meetings at least once monthly

Consequences

District 3350 reserves the right to terminate an exchange at any time. Any cost related to early return shall be the responsibility of the student's natural parents.

If found guilty of violating Thai law, you will receive NO assistance from Rotary or your home country's government and will be returned home as soon as released by authorities.

Important: These rules are designed for your safety and the success of your exchange. Breaking them can result in early termination of your exchange program.

Thai Language Resources

Thai is a tonal language with five tones: monotone, low, falling, high, and rising. While it can be challenging, learning even basic phrases will greatly enhance your exchange experience. English is widely understood in Bangkok, but less so in other regions.

Essential Thai Phrases

สวัสดีค่ะ / ครับ Sawasdee ka / krab Hello (ka for female / krab for male)
ผมชื่อ ____ (male) Pom cheu ____ My name is ____
ฉันชื่อ ____ (female) Chan cheu ____ My name is ____
ยินดีที่ได้รู้จักค่ะ / ครับ Yin dee tee dai roo jag ka / krab Nice to meet you
คุณสบายดีไหม Kun sabai dee mai How are you?
ฉันสบายดีค่ะ / ครับ Chan sabai dee ka / krab I am fine thank you
ขอบคุณค่ะ / ครับ Kob kun ka / krab Thank you
คุณมาจากประเทศไหน Kun ma jak prates nai? Where do you come from?
ฉันมาจากประเทศ ____ Chan ma jag prates ____ I come from ______
ฉันเป็นนักเรียนแลกเปลี่ยนโรตารี Chan pen nag rien lak plean rotary I am a Rotary Youth Exchange Student

Numbers in Thai

1 - หนึ่ง nueng
2 - สอง song
3 - สาม sarm
4 - สี่ see
5 - ห้า ha
6 - หก hok
7 - เจ็ด jed
8 - แปด paed
9 - เก้า kao
10 - สิบ sib

Numbers 11-19 use "sib-" + number (e.g., 11 = sib-ed, 12 = sib-song)
